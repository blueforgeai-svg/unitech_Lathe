§ 01 · The storyA workshop on Gondal Road

One workshop. Three generations.

Uni-Tech Machines was founded in 1986 by Mr. Kiranbhai Vachhani — a single-bay workshop on Gondal Road, Rajkot, turning rollers for a local mill. Forty years on, the workshop has grown into a seventy-eight-model production catalog covering lathes, hydraulic presses, drills, slotters, shapers, bandsaws and milling machines — but the bay is still there.

We are a sole proprietorship with twenty-six to fifty skilled engineers and technicians on the floor. Every machine that leaves our gate is bolted, aligned, run-in and signed off by a named engineer. No exceptions. No shortcuts. No mystery.

In 2009 we were certified to ISO 9001:2008, and the same quality-management discipline runs across every SKU. We hold GST 24ADLPV8104E1ZI, and ship FOB / CIF Mundra to twelve export markets across the Gulf, East Africa and South-East Asia.

Customers include Indian Sugar Corp, paper-mill operators, rolling-steel plants, the oil & gas sector, tool rooms, and the dozens of contract workshops who keep India's heavy industry turning.

§ 04 · PrinciplesThe way we build

The way we
build.

Three principles. Forty years. Every machine that ships passes through all three. The thread that holds the catalog together.

I

Bolted, aligned,
run-in.

Every machine is fully assembled, dial-aligned, broken-in and signed off by a named engineer before it leaves Plot 04. No corner cut between the gate and the lorry.

II

Cast iron,
seasoned hard.

Beds are flame-hardened. Castings are seasoned for stability before machining. The result is a machine that holds its line under load for thirty years.

III

A name on
every tag.

Behind every machine that ships is a named engineer who's accountable for it — even ten years later. That's how a workshop runs for forty years on one plot of land.

§ 05 · TimelineSelected milestones · 1986–2026

Forty years,
a hundred milestones.

A selected timeline — the moments that shaped the workshop. Bays added, certifications gained, machines that went on to define a series.

1986
Workshop founded by Kiranbhai Vachhani
A single bay on Gondal Road, light-duty lathe contracts for Rajkot mills — one cone-pulley lathe and three apprentices.
PLOT 04 · RAJKOT
1992
Heavy-duty series launched
First 7-foot heavy-duty lathe (UT/HD-7) ships to a steel rolling mill in Bhavnagar. The series goes on to become a workshop staple.
UT/HD-7 · STEEL
1998
First 20-foot plano-type bed
Indian Sugar Corp orders a 20-foot plano-type lathe for roller turning. Plot 04's bay had to be extended to fit the machine.
UT/PL-16 · SUGAR
2009
ISO 9001:2008 certification
Quality management system rolled out across every SKU. Named engineers, signed off, with traceable batch records.
ISO 9001:2008
2018
First export — Nigeria
Heavy-duty lathes shipped to the West-African oil sector. The export desk has since opened twelve markets.
